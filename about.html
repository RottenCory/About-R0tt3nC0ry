My Identity

I am Non-human on every level. Hear me out, let me explain. My Alterhuman Identity: 1. My identity is literally non‑human This isn’t metaphorical or aesthetic — it’s the most accurate description of how I exist internally. I’m not claiming supernatural traits or physical transformation. I’m describing an internal identity that is consistent, literal, and non‑human. I align with a holothere identity: identifying fully and fundamentally as something outside the human category. 2. My identity has three non‑material components I experience myself as something that doesn’t fit into a physical species. It’s closer to how concepts like void or air “exist.” Core components: Voidkin — tied to darkness, absence, emptiness Astralkin — tied to a non‑physical or non‑material form Conceptkin — identity rooted in an abstract concept rather than biology These aren’t roles or aesthetics — they’re foundational parts of how I exist. 3. How I relate to gender I often say I have no gender, which is partly true. Some aspects of my Conceptkin identity include: Roadkill gender Canine gender These shape how I experience myself, but not in a human‑gender framework. I also use I/me and we/us interchangeably. This isn’t because I’m a system — it’s because I experience myself and my vessel as distinct, and acknowledging that difference feels grounding and positive. 4. My void‑canine form My internal form is a void‑canine — a large, intelligent working‑dog‑like being made of darkness and non‑material presence. General sense of form: Built similarly to a Belgian Malinois or wolfdog Entirely black, light‑absorbing, more “presence” than physical creature Light softens around me, looking cut‑out‑of‑reality (literally.) Key features: Tall, precise ears Bright white eyes Short, shifting dark “fur” Long, powerful legs Canine paws and expressive tail A void‑like aura when not fully formed I also have butterfly‑like antennae used for sensory input; they fold back when not needed. Sometimes I appear as a normal dog, but my true form is void‑born — not a regular animal. I use canine biological terms because they fit my identity better than human ones, even if I don’t always use them openly due to judgment. 5. Void‑canine internal experience Internally, my void‑canine body feels non‑material. My flesh is something like rotting, decaying void matter, that slowly regenerates itself. The sense of internal decay connects to: Roadkill gender Themes of death, absence, and transformation A sense of kinship with certain objects, creatures, and symbols These connections are identity‑based and familial, not harmful or inappropriate. Some things feel familiar or “related” to me. not because I am those things, but because they feel like family within my identity. I’m firmly: anti‑harm anti‑abuse anti‑exploitation anti‑contact in any harmful or inappropriate sense I don’t use certain labels for myself, but the concepts help explain how my identity works. 6. I know I’m perceived as human I’m fully aware of how my vessel appears. But external perception doesn’t define internal identity. My vessel is human‑shaped. My internal self isn’t. Those two realities coexist, and I function normally in daily life. This isn’t escapism — it's an accurate self‑description. 7. Simplest summary I’m a non‑human being in a human‑shaped vessel. My identity is void‑based, astral, conceptual, and canine. I don’t claim physical transformation — only a non‑human internal experience.
